    Bio

    Cassidy Lamm is a senior at the University of Alabama majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Studio Art. She is currently supported by an NSF and College Board grant that is investigating the context for a new Advanced Placement exam in collaboration with the College Board.

    Artwork

    Fabric on Bamboo


    Media: Charcoal
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from a dark fabric being thrown over a piece of bamboo. The texture of the fabric is manifested as the material has a soft yet dense quality. The fabric also appears heavy which contrasts the width of the small bamboo stick that supports it. The values lean more towards darker shades rather lighter tints, highlighting the darker color of the original fabric.

    Dress on Mannequin


    Media: Charcoal
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from a dress on a mannequin. The body of the dress has three distinct textures: a plaited material around the shoulders and bust line, a smooth and sturdy midsection, and folds in the bottom section that are formed by pleats. The thicker lines in the top section fade through the midsection as the smoother texture becomes more apparent. Each fold of the bottom section is formed by a pleat or combination of pleats and is manifested in the various values.

    Bottles on a Flag


    Media: Charcoal
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from an American flag being thrown over a box and placing various sized glass bottles on top. The various values of the flag reflect the lighting of the room as spotlights were casted on selected areas of the still life. When viewed through one of the glass bottles, the stripes of the flag are contorted, becoming wavy and changing direction.

    Cloaked Fabric


    Media: Charcoal
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from a dark fabric being thrown over a post structure. The top of the post is represented by the tip of the fabric and how it flows down and out from that single point. The values manifest the smooth texture of the material and the various folds and curves that the underlying structure has caused it to form. There is also a hemline that lies an inch above the bottom of the material that can be followed throughout various areas of the piece.

    White Still Life


    Media: Graphite Pencil
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from an arrangement of white objects placed on top of a white sheet on a table. The darker shades appear where certain objects cast shadows on other objects as well as the negative space in the background. The lighter tints appear where the spotlights highlighted certain parts of the objects.

    Two Folds


    Media: Charcoal
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in.
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was also drawn from the posted structure of the “Cloaked Fabric”, however, it is drawn from a different viewpoint. In this piece, the values are the darkest inside the various folds and in the slope between the two posts. The texture is smooth, similar to the previous viewpoint of this structure.

    Shaded Still Life


    Media: Graphite Pencil
    Size: 18 in. x 24 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from an arrangement of various objects with various colors. There is a broad range of values of this piece, from the darkest shades on the black mannequin on the left to the lightest tints on the highlights of the skull and white mannequin. Each object also has a different texture. The box on the far right appears grainy, while the top edges of the white mannequin are jagged.

    Multi-textured Still Life


    Media: Charcoal
    Size: 22 in. x 30 in
    Date: Fall 2012
    Description: This piece is a still life that was drawn from an arrangement of large objects, all with varied textures, patterns, materials, and values. Rather than draw the entirety of the still life, we were required to focus on a smaller section. The large patterned fabric on the right side is the back of an old dress. There is also a faded leather suitcase, glassware, reflective aluminum containers and a paper flower. The reflection of the flower pots depicts the classroom and the various students sitting at their individual drawing boards with the lights in the ceiling above us. The leather suitcase is cracking and is also partially reflective on one side.

    Stairs








    Media: Blue Painter's Tape
    Size: approx. 9 ft. x 7 ft.
    Date: Summer 2014
    Description: This piece was made solely with blue painter’s tape, scissors, and an X-ACTO knife. The object was to create a design in the stairway using a unique media (i.e. tape). My goal in this piece was to play off the location of a stairwell and make it seem like whoever was walking the stairs could go multiple directions. This was accomplished by extending the physical staircase visually and having it extend upwards into a door. There are also several staircases that stem from the main stairs or go beneath it. In addition to bringing the two-dimensional to life, I also experimented with three-dimensional on the right side of the piece. Instead of limiting the tape to the wall itself, I allowed it to connect the two walls by forming a truly three-dimensional staircase that pops out from the wall and ends as a two-dimensional staircase.
